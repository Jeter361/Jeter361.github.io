About Me

I was born and raised in Norfolk, Virginia, where my passion for technology started at a young age. While playing games online, I ran into modders, and that experience sparked my interest in cybersecurity. At 13, I attended the GenCyber summer camp at Old Dominion University — the youngest participant there — and it further fueled my curiosity about computers and security.

Growing up, I loved experimenting with computers, breaking things apart and learning how to fix them. I started building and selling gaming PCs, which gave me both hands-on technical experience and an entrepreneurial mindset. Outside of tech, I stayed active by playing varsity baseball and diving at Maury High School.

Today, I’m pursuing a Cybersecurity degree at Old Dominion University while working in IT on campus. I’ve gained practical experience fixing a wide range of problems on Windows machines — internal and external. I’ve seen more blue screens than I can count and replaced even more CMOS batteries, but those challenges taught me how to troubleshoot under pressure and find creative solutions.

I love the challenge of solving problems, learning new technologies, and building a career that blends my passion for technology with real-world impact.
